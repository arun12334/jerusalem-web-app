Does Unity Require Baptism?

Ephesians 4 teaches that followers of Christ should strive to keep "the unity of the Spirit in the bond of peace," emphasizing humility, patience, love, and one faith centered in Jesus Christ.

In John 3, Jesus taught Nicodemus that being born of water and of the Spirit is essential to enter the kingdom of God. Likewise, repentance and baptism are sacred principles taught throughout scripture.

Acts 19 records disciples who had received John's baptism and were later instructed more fully concerning Jesus Christ. Their experience reminds us that God guides His children according to His perfect wisdom.

Building Zion begins with faith, repentance, charity, and a willingness to follow the Lord. Every individual should prayerfully seek God's direction while showing love and respect to others.

Loving God first and loving our neighbors creates lasting unity. Through Christ, people from many backgrounds can become one in purpose and one in heart.
